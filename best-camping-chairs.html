Best Camping Chairs for Relaxing in Style

 Posted on August 30, 2024  |  20 minutes  |  4225 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

It may be tempting to grab a cheap, collapsing chair from the bargain bin of your local sporting goods store, but when you’re stargazing by the campfire with your friends and family, the last thing you should worry about is a sore back, while fighting the urge to abandon an uncomfortable chair in the woods. Whether fishing by the lake for an afternoon, hiking to the farthest end of the trail, or exploring parts unknown, you deserve a much better seat wherever your adventures take you.

Nature lovers need a quality camping chair that’s easy to store and carry with your gear, will easily survive the elements, and provides relaxing, body-supporting comfort to recharge you for the next leg of the journey. With so many camping chairs on the market—ranging from expensive, luxury ergonomic designs to the fairly-priced yet feature-packed models, and everything in between—looking for the best camping chair for your needs can be overwhelming.

After extensive research and many hours of hands-on testing in the wild, we’ve compiled a list of the all-around greatest camping chairs available, with plenty of key tips to ensure that your next camping trip is that much cozier.

What to consider when choosing a camping chair

Portability and weight

If you’re pulling a set of camping chairs from the back of your truck or RV after driving onto your campsite, you shouldn’t worry about how bulky or heavy your favorite seat is, as long as it’s reliably comfortable and incorporates the features you need.

On the other hand, anyone hiking with their camping gear will want chairs made of sturdy, yet featherweight aluminum to drastically lighten the overall load. These premium camping chairs are often collapsible, easy to set up in under a minute, and include a handy, mini carrying case for maximum portability. The minimal downsides of a lightweight camping chair are that they’re easily blown over on a breezy day and may seem relatively unstable when compared to larger steel-framed chairs at first, but seasoned adventurers will appreciate the feel of a 1.2-pound chair in their pack over a 7-pound model anytime.

Comfort and support

Just because you’re out in the wilderness doesn’t mean you have to sprawl on the ground like an animal. Generally speaking, the pricier a camping chair is, the more comfortable it will be—bottom of the barrel camping chairs are dirt cheap, but tend to be boxy, flimsy, and provide minimal support.

Some of the key points to look for when looking for a particularly comfortable camping chair include the ability to recline, a headrest, an elevated seat, and adjustable armrests. In addition, it’s worth considering a chair’s width, shape, seat height, flexibility, weight capacity, and whether or not it has a cozy ergonomic design to ensure it will be a good fit for your body type. Campers who need a deluxe chair may be interested in premium comfort options such as a heated seat or cozy rocking feature.

Keep in mind that some of the most comfortable camping chairs are also relatively heavy, so be weary of models with a foldable steel frame or a bulky design if you need a portable camping chair.

Durability and materials

The best camping chairs are as durable as they are comfortable, so they can easily withstand the elements for years of enjoying the great outdoors. Most chairs are lined with a polyester fabric, since this material is lightweight, surprisingly rugged, and weather resistant, but nylon and canvas are solid alternatives.

Both aluminum and steel chair frames are extremely durable, but aluminum models are much lighter and easier to pack for hiking expeditions. Steel camping chairs are the more durable of the two, but are better suited for short walks from your car to the campsite as they’re much heavier and more cumbersome than their aluminum counterparts.

Ease of use

Comfort and weight are the two most important factors when selecting a camping chair, but usability is a key point to consider as well. Some of the lightest camping chairs were made to be fully disassembled and stored in a bag between uses, but many of these essentially assemble themselves thanks to the elastic, shock-corded frame literally pulling itself together after you’ve pulled it out of its bag. Alternatively, some models fold down like beach chairs, while others collapse inward to be stowed in the slim shoulder bag it came with.

The ability to quickly unpack and set up your trusty camping chair as soon as you need to take a load off is incredibly convenient, and it’s worth looking for video reviews online to see how simple a chair you’re considering is to assemble and take down.

Additional features

Comfort is king, but extras like dual cup holders, mesh storage pockets for your gear, a convenient carrying bag, and a range of cool color options to choose from are certainly attractive bonus features to keep an eye out for when comparing camping chairs.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the best material for camping chairs?

Although you’ll find a few models made with canvas or a signature synthetic blend, the vast majority of camping chairs utilize polyester fabric in their designs. Ripstop polyester is difficult to cut or tear by accident, is water and weather resistant, and notably lightweight, making it a popular pick for camping gear manufacturers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Which brand of folding chair is the best?

The Lay-Z Lounger, from Alps Mountaineering, is a particularly impressive model due to its wide range of reclining positions, especially durable steel frame, and how simple it is to fold down for storage. While it’s on the heavier side for a camping chair, the Lay-Z Lounger’s comfortable canvas material and plush headrest makes it surprisingly easy to nod off once you’ve found your optimum reclining angle.

Are Helinox chairs worth it?

If you’re a dedicated camper, absolutely! Helinox’s camping chairs are known for their featherweight aluminum alloy frames, which makes them both incredibly durable and much easier to hike with when compared to bulkier, steel models. In addition, expect a Helinox chair to utilize reinforced polyester for a more comfortable feel and enhanced durability, as well as an extremely easy to assemble design so you can get off your feet that much faster.

As an added bonus, many Helinox camping chairs are offered in a rainbow of eye-catching colors, so you can find a cozy chair that matches your style.

Which chair material is best for sitting for a long time?

Like backpacks and tents, many camping chairs are made of lightweight and durable polyester, so it’s important to look for ergonomically designed models if you’re in the market for a quality chair to lounge in nature for hours on end.

When you’re comparing models, look for chairs touting features like an adjustable headrest and armrests, as well as the ability to recline. These key points make it easy for campers to customize their chair to better fit their body type.

How much should I spend on a good chair?

There is certainly a bit of truth to the phrase, “You get what you pay for,” so when it comes to camping chairs, expect to pay anywhere from at least $80 to around $170 for a genuinely good chair that is comfortable, feature-rich, and built to last.

Cheaper chairs may be tempting, but they won’t last nearly as long in the elements and are half as comfortable as pricier counterparts. If you plan on spending a lot of time on the beach or in the woods, it’s well worth investing in a superior model anyway.
