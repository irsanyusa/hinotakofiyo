If you have a new mom or dad in your life it can feel impossible to find a way to make their lives easier as they deal with a lack of sleep, around-the-clock baby-caring duties and never-ending dirty diapers, in addition to all of the normal stressors of daily life.

Thankfully, there are more products than ever before designed to make a new parent’s life just a little bit easier.

Hatch Baby Sound Machine

The hype is real on the Hatch. First up it’s wifi enabled so you can control the sound and volume from different rooms. It’s touch sensitive so all you need to do to turn it on and off is tap. Plus the colorful nightlight option can be used for older kids as you start to sleep train. Plenty of parents will set the nightlight on a certain color and remind their little ones that they need to stay in bed until the light turns green. Thus ending early morning wake ups. If this price is a bit too much, you can always get the also reliable yogasleep sound machine, which is perfect for travel. Honestly, we have both.

Of all the things we used in the baby-to-toddler phase, this was the item we treasured the most. These little coolers can fit in anything (a purse, diaper bag, clipped to the outside of your stroller). Plus they flip inside failry easily for cleaning and are made out of an easily wipeable and stain-resistant material. It usually can fit around 2-3 standard size 4 oz bottles upright, or 2 standard size 8 oz bottles, packed sideways. When in doubt, give the gift of food. It doesn’t have to be fancy or even fresh (sometimes frozen is better) but send eats. There are so many fantastic options to send food to new parents online. Williams Sonoma offers three nights of frozen entrees you can have shipped to their door including lasagna, meatloaf and a pot pie. There are loads of vegan and vegetarian options on there as well, but we’re super partial to the frozen ready-to-bake chocolate croissants. And if there’s a regional delicacy you can send, even better. Scour Gold Belly and see what favorite treats you could send from their hometown.
