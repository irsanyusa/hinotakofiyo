America’s Got Talent Season 18 returns on Tuesday night with the fourth live show of the season, as 11 more acts take the stage for America’s vote. Only two acts will make it to the finale during Wednesday’s results show.

AGT has already shared which acts will be performing on this week’s episode, airing on NBC. Read on to find out who this week’s acts are and revisit their impressive auditions from earlier in the season.

Who’s Performing on AGT Tonight?

Anna DeGuzman

This talented performer shared her goal to inspire more young women to pursue magic. She wowed the judges with her card tricks, admitting that she “didn’t think it was gonna work.” The panel chose to send Anna to the live shows.

Chibi Unity

This dance group from Japan impressed the judges and audience with their amazingly in-sync audition. The performance earned this season’s group Golden Buzzer from the panel and host Terry Crews, sending them straight to the AGT live shows.

D’Corey Johnson

This 11-year-old singer stunned in his audition with a powerhouse performance of “Open Arms” by Journey. The judges loved it so much, they chose to send D’Corey to the Season 18 live shows. Now he’s competing for a spot in the Finals.

Freedom Singers

This singing group from Skid Row is made up of people who have faced homelessness. For their audition, they performed a gospel version of “Under the Bridge” by the Red Hot Chili Peppers. The judges gave them a standing ovation and chose to send the group to the live shows.

Kylie Frey

This self-described “rodeo queen” performed an original tribute to her late grandfather during her Season 18 audition. Three out of the four judges chose to send Kylie through, and now she’s competing in the live shows for a spot in the finale.

Mariandrea

This 14-year-old dancer from Mexico wowed the panel with her fierce audition, which involved some seriously creepy moves. The judges chose to send Mariandrea to the live shows, where she’s competing for a spot in the Finals.

Orlando Leyba

This comedian made the audience laugh with his audition, in which he joked about his love for the show Yellowstone. The judges got a kick out of Orlando’s set, so they chose to send him to the Season 18 live shows.

Puppet Simon and the Cow Belles

The judges weren’t sure what to make of this puppet act at first, but when Simon Cowell saw a puppet version of himself, he was won over. The voice of that puppet was later revealed to be AGT alum Gabriel Brown. Now the fun act is headed to the live shows.

Roland Abante

This singer from the Philippines got emotional in his audition as he shared his dream with the judges. Roland went on to sing “When a Man Loves a Woman” by Michael Bolton. His soulful voice inspired the judges to send him to the live shows.

Shadow Ace

This performer from the Philippines said he wants to revive shadow art. His creative audition made the judges laugh and earned a standing ovation. Now he’s competing in the live shows for a chance to appear in the finale.

Zion Clark

This inspiring contestant was born without legs and grew up in foster care. He went on to become a successful wrestler. For his audition, Zion performed acrobatic moves while sharing his life story. The judges chose to send him to the Season 18 live shows.
